Assumption: **PSF = Problem–Solution Fit**, **BMs = business models**, **BMC = Business Model Canvas**. Your idea is: **a professor-led R&D expert tool/service that rapidly checks technologies, tests performance, and launches small POCs/MVPs for customers.**

Core principle: start with **small, measurable MVPs**, because Lean Startup emphasizes solving a real problem first, then using MVPs and build–measure–learn feedback loops to validate learning quickly. ([The Lean Startup](https://theleanstartup.com/principles?utm_source=chatgpt.com "The Lean Startup | Methodology")) For technology assessment, use a maturity scale like **TRL 1–9**, where TRL measures how mature a technology is, from early principles to proven operational use. ([NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/directorates/somd/space-communications-navigation-program/technology-readiness-levels/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Technology Readiness Levels"))

## 1. Sharp positioning

**Best positioning:**  
**“Independent R&D validation lab for companies that need to decide fast whether a technology is worth buying, building, funding, or killing.”**

Do **not** position first as a generic “AI/tech consulting firm.” Too broad.

Better wedge:

> “We help technical and business leaders validate emerging technologies in 2–6 weeks through expert review, benchmarks, POCs, and MVPs.”

## 3. Best early customer segment

Start with **Corporate R&D / innovation teams** and **technical SMEs**.

Why: they already have budget, real problems, and urgency. They also value credibility from a **CS professor as lead**. ## 7. Example problem–solution fits to test first

### PSF A: Corporate AI confusion

**Problem:** “We have 20 AI ideas but do not know which one is valuable or feasible.”  
**Solution:** 2-week AI use-case validation sprint.  
**Test:** interview 10 CTOs/innovation leads.  
**Buying signal:** they pay for a €5k–€10k diagnostic.

### PSF B: Vendor selection risk

**Problem:** “Every vendor claims their tool is best; we need independent proof.”  
**Solution:** benchmark 2–4 vendors against customer data and requirements.  
**Test:** offer one benchmark report to three companies.  
**Buying signal:** procurement or R&D budget pays.

### PSF C: Startup technical credibility

**Problem:** “Investors/customers do not trust our technical claims.”  
**Solution:** independent professor-led validation report.  
**Test:** sell to 5 deep-tech/AI startups.  
**Buying signal:** founders pay or investors request it.

### PSF D: Research commercialization

**Problem:** “We have research but no customer-ready MVP.”  
**Solution:** convert research into demo + commercial use-case test.  
**Test:** partner with one university lab.  
**Buying signal:** grant/commercialization office funds sprint.

## 8. What to build first

Build **not a full platform**, but a repeatable service toolkit:

1. **Technology checker scorecard**  
    Criteria: maturity, performance, security, integration effort, cost, IP risk, data needs, business value.
    
2. **Benchmark template**  
    Input/output tests, latency, cost, accuracy, robustness, maintainability.
    
3. **POC playbook**  
    Problem definition → success metric → prototype → test → decision.
    
4. **Executive report format**  
    One-page decision: **build / buy / partner / pause / kill**.
    
5. **Landing page with 3 offers**  
    “Tech Reality Check,” “POC Sprint,” “Investor Tech DD.”
    

## 9. Recommended go-to-market

Start narrow:

**Target:** CTOs, heads of innovation, R&D managers in industrial SMEs and tech startups.  
**Message:** “Stop wasting months on weak POCs. We validate technologies in weeks.”  
**First sales motion:** professor-led workshop → paid diagnostic → POC sprint → retainer.

## 10. Best initial business model

The strongest starting model is:

> **Expert-led R&D Validation Studio**

Not SaaS first. Not marketplace first. Not broad consulting.

Start with paid sprints, collect repeated patterns, then turn the internal methodology into software later.

**Best first revenue stack:**

1. €5k–€10k **Technology Reality Check**
    
2. €20k–€50k **POC/MVP Sprint**
    
3. €8k–€25k/month **R&D Retainer**
    
4. Later: subscription benchmark platform
    

## 11. Key risks

|Risk|Mitigation|
|---|---|
|Too broad positioning|Pick one wedge: AI/automation/enterprise tech validation.|
|Professor brand too academic|Emphasize business outcomes, not only research quality.|
|Service not scalable|Standardize scorecards, reports, benchmarks, reusable tools.|
|Long enterprise sales cycles|Start with SMEs, startups, VCs, innovation teams.|
|POCs do not convert|Define success metrics before building.|

## Final recommendation

Launch with this offer:

**“Professor-led Technology Validation Sprint: in 2–4 weeks we test whether your target technology is technically feasible, commercially useful, and worth scaling.”**

Best first customers: **corporate R&D teams, technical SMEs, startups needing validation, and investors doing technical due diligence.**  
Best first monetization: **fixed-fee diagnostics + POC/MVP sprints**, then move into **retainers and benchmark SaaS** once patterns repeat.

## Die besten ersten Zielkunden

Ich würde nicht bei allen gleichzeitig starten. Die attraktivsten Segmente sind:

### 1. Mittelständische Unternehmen mit digitalem Druck

Zum Beispiel Maschinenbau, Logistik, Healthcare-Dienstleister, Energie, Versicherungsnahe Dienstleister. Sie haben viele repetitive Wissensprozesse, Dokumente, Supportfragen, interne Daten und Fachwissen — aber oft keine starke AI-R&D-Abteilung.

**Kaufgrund:**  
„Wir wissen, AI ist wichtig, aber wir wissen nicht, welcher Use Case wirklich lohnt.“

**Verkaufsangebot:**  
**AI Opportunity & POC Sprint**  
In 2–3 Wochen: Use Cases finden, priorisieren, einen funktionierenden POC bauen.

### 2. Softwarefirmen mit AI-Roadmap

Diese Kunden verstehen Tech und zahlen schneller, wenn du nachweisen kannst, dass du Geschwindigkeit oder Qualität erhöhst.

**Kaufgrund:**  
„Wir müssen AI-Features liefern, aber unser Team ist ausgelastet.“

**Verkaufsangebot:**  
**AI Feature Acceleration Sprint**  
Chatbot, Copilot, Agent, Semantic Search, Code Assistant, Recommendation Engine, Optimierungsalgorithmus.

### 3. Beratungen und Agenturen als Partner

Viele Beratungen verkaufen AI-Strategie, aber haben nicht genug echte Builder. Du kannst als technischer Deep-Tech-Partner auftreten.

**Kaufgrund:**  
„Wir brauchen jemanden, der das tatsächlich baut.“

**Verkaufsangebot:**  
**White-label AI Engineering Partner**  
Du baust POCs/MVPs im Hintergrund, die Beratung hält die Kundenbeziehung.

## Geschäftsmodell

Ich würde mit **productized consulting** starten, nicht sofort mit SaaS.

Phase 1: **Dienstleistung mit klaren Paketen**  
Technology Check, POC Sprint, MVP Sprint.

Phase 2: **Wiederverwendbare Assets bauen**  
RAG-Starterkit, Evaluation Framework, Agent Templates, Security Checklist, Prompt/Test Suites.

Phase 3: **Retainer und Plattform**  
Kunden zahlen monatlich für kontinuierliches AI-R&D, Monitoring neuer Technologien, kleine Experimente und MVP-Erweiterungen.

Phase 4: **Softwareprodukt**  
Aus wiederkehrenden Projekten entsteht eventuell ein eigenes „Technology Checker / POC Automation Tool“.

## Der beste Einstiegssatz für Sales

> „Welche AI- oder Tech-Idee liegt bei Ihnen seit Monaten auf dem Tisch, weil niemand Zeit hat, sie sauber zu testen? Wir bauen Ihnen in 10 Arbeitstagen einen belastbaren POC und sagen ehrlich, ob es sich lohnt.“

Das ist stark, weil es nicht Hype verkauft, sondern Entscheidungssicherheit.

1. **AI / Tech Feasibility Check** — günstiger Einstieg, 3.000–10.000 €.
